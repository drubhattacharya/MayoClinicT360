Access to Care — Appointment Delay & Transportation Barriers
Priority #1 · 34% delayed care · 15% no PCP · disability + foreign-born most affected
High NEMT Relevance►

Key data: 34% of adults delayed care. Top reason: “not being able to get an appointment.” Transportation compounds this for residents with disabilities (9.7%) and foreign-born residents (11.1%). PCP gap improved from 22% in 2018 to 15% in 2024, but 15% of 164,784 = ~24,700 without a primary care provider.

NEMT connection: Each prevented transport-related no-show directly addresses CHNA Priority #1. SDOH Z-code documentation (Z75.3 — Transportation Barrier) creates a simultaneous evidence chain for IRS Schedule H and CRA community development classification.

Mayo advantage: FindHelp is already embedded in Mayo’s EHR (added 2022). NEMT enrollment can be built into the existing FindHelp workflow — reducing care coordinator burden and accelerating the documentation trail examiners need.

Source: 2025 Mayo Clinic in Rochester CHNA Report · 2024 Olmsted County Community Survey (N=937) · Access to Care indicator
🧠
Mental Health — Persistent Priority, Renter & Disability Concentration
Priority #2 · Persistent across 2022 + 2025 · Depression rate exceeds state & national benchmarks
Med. NEMT Relevance►

Key data: 37% of adults reported mental health issues in 2024 — up 3% since 2021. Olmsted depression rate 28% vs. 23% (MN) and 22% (U.S.). Higher disparities among renters — a strong proxy for lower income and reduced vehicle access. Mayo invested $4M+ in SERCC and $7.5M+ in mental health nonprofits in the 2022–2025 cycle.

NEMT connection: Behavioral health appointments have among the highest transport-attributable no-show rates of any specialty. NEMT for renter/disability populations for behavioral health visits addresses both Access (#1) and Mental Health (#2) simultaneously — dual-priority Schedule H documentation from a single program investment.

CRA framing: Community development services for LMI populations addressing mental health access qualify under 12 CFR 25.04(c)(3). Serving two CHNA priorities in one program produces enhanced narrative strength for the exam file.

Source: 2025 Mayo Clinic CHNA · Mental Health indicator · Rochester Epidemiology Project · MN Dept. of Health BRFSS
🍑
Food Security — New 2025 Priority · Co-occurring SDOH · CRA Basic Needs Bridge
Priority #3 · Newly identified · Channel One: 23% Hispanic, 14% Black
Med. NEMT Relevance►

Key data: 33,000 Channel One Food Bank clients in 2024. 8% of adults worried about running out of food. Food security rate 92% county-wide (vs. 90% MN / 86% national) but 2% lower than 2020. Client demographics: 23% Hispanic, 14% Black, 11% Asian — all populations with elevated transportation barriers.

NEMT bundling: At Mayo’s operational scale, NEMT trip routing can include a Channel One pickup/delivery stop — creating three-priority documentation (Access + Mental Health + Food Security) from a single investment. A design opportunity unique to a health system with Mayo’s existing Channel One relationship.

CRA framing: “Basic needs” category under CRA community development definitions. A bank investment addressing all three 2025 CHNA priorities creates the strongest multi-priority CRA narrative available in the Rochester MSA Assessment Area.

Source: 2025 Mayo Clinic CHNA · Food Security indicator · Channel One Food Bank 2024 · Feeding America
🔬
Rochester Epidemiology Project — Mayo’s Unique Attribution Infrastructure
Mayo-only asset · Longitudinal data since 1966 · 56M+ patient records in Mayo Platform
Unique to Mayo►

What it is: The Rochester Epidemiology Project (REP) is a population-based data linkage system covering Olmsted County residents, operated with Mayo Clinic since 1966. The 2025 CHNA explicitly used REP to show health indicator trends — a methodological advantage no other local health system can replicate.

NEMT attribution: REP’s linked records enable NEMT outcomes studies with longitudinal population controls unavailable elsewhere. A propensity-matched cohort study produces publication-quality evidence at a fraction of a de novo RCT cost — transforming the program from a community service into a real-world evidence platform.

Board framing: “This is not just a transportation program — it is a peer-reviewed publication opportunity that distinguishes Mayo’s community benefit strategy nationally and documents ROI with the methodological rigor Mayo Clinic’s research reputation demands.”

Source: Rochester Epidemiology Project (mayoclinic.org) · Mayo Clinic Platform (56M+ records) · 2025 CHNA process description
